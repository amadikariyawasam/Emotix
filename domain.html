Research Abstract

EMOTIX: AI System for Employee Assistant and Mental Wellness

EMOTIX is an intelligent, privacy-conscious smart workplace ecosystem designed to enhance employee wellness, concentration, stress awareness, and system security in contemporary online workspaces. The system combines several artificial intelligence modules, such as adaptive routine monitoring using TinyML, focus detection using computer vision, stress recognition using deep learning, AI chatbot support, and cybersecurity protection by deception. The adaptive routine module trains individual work patterns locally on Raspberry Pi edge devices, providing privacy by processing on-site and updating routines with human-in-the-loop consent. Focus detection employs eye tracking, blink rate analysis, and environmental sensing to produce real-time focus scores. Simultaneously, a safe stress detection pipeline based on CNN and LSTM models tracks facial emotion trends to calculate dynamic stress scores through valence-arousal mapping. Protected by AES-256-GCM encryption and an AI-based honeypot, EMOTIX offers a human-friendly, scalable, and secure platform for intelligent workplace wellness support.

Keywords — Workplace wellness, TinyML, focus detection, stress recognition, computer vision, CNN-LSTM, AI chatbot, honeypot security, edge computing, privacy-preserving AI.
01
Literature Survey

What Came Before EMOTIX

The fast expansion of digital workplaces has had a profound impact on how employees relate with technology. A rise in screen time, cognitive load, and multitasking have been cited as significant contributors to decreasing focus and increasing stress. Traditional wellness platforms rely on predetermined notifications and wearable biosensors that are not always able to capture evolving individual work patterns.

Embedded Monitoring

Recent research favors adaptive machine learning systems that autonomously model behavior. However, many existing systems update routines without approval, causing privacy and trust issues.

Computer Vision Focus

Older systems monitor attendance but not true focus. Modern approaches like MediaPipeFaceMesh allow gaze tracking and blink detection to measure fatigue and distraction.

Affective Computing

Stress detection has moved from invasive wearables (EEG/ECG) to non-invasive facial emotion recognition using CNN and LSTM networks for temporal analysis.

Conversational Assistants

Context-sensitive chatbots have been demonstrated to enhance interaction by transforming technical analytics into comprehensible wellness suggestions.

02
Research Gap

What's Still Missing

Existing systems lack real-time mental wellness monitoring with privacy-preserving local processing. No current platform combines adaptive routine learning, attention tracking, temporal stress detection, and conversational AI support in one ecosystem while also ensuring data security.

Most CNN-based stress systems classify single frames without temporal context; environmental data is almost never fused into focus models; and no affective monitoring system pairs AES-256-GCM encryption with HMAC-SHA256 packet-level authentication as a unified transmission security layer. EMOTIX closes this gap by offering adaptive monitoring with human-in-the-loop consent.

Research Problem

The Problem EMOTIX Solves

Modern workplaces create mental exhaustion and decreased focus. EMOTIX addresses five core failure modes of existing systems:

☁️ Privacy Violations

Cloud-based platforms expose sensitive biometric data without adequate protection.

🎭 Incomplete Assessment

Systems track only presence — not actual mental states or wellness.

🔓 Security Gaps

Monitoring infrastructure leaves employee data vulnerable to cyber threats.

⏱️ No Real-Time Support

Wellness recommendations are delayed, generic, and not personalized.

👁️ Employee Distrust

Invasive monitoring without data protection destroys workforce trust.

05
Methodology

How EMOTIX Works

A modular experimental system design involving AI, computer vision, TinyML, and cybersecurity:

A. Data Acquisition: Facial expression, eye movements, and environmental variables (light, noise, temperature) are captured via non-obtrusive sensors.
B. Adaptive Routine (TinyML): Raspberry Pi logs presence timestamps for 2 weeks to build a baseline. The model uses μ ± max(1.5σ, 45min) to detect behavioral shifts.
C. Focus Detection: MediaPipe identifies landmarks for EAR (blink rate) and gaze estimation. A weighted formula computes focus scores in real-time.
D. AI Chatbot: A rule-based engine converts analytical outputs into personalized break reminders and workspace adjustment suggestions.
E. Stress Detection (CNN-LSTM): CNN classifies emotions; an LSTM analyzes transitions across a 15-frame sliding buffer to predict stress via valence-arousal mapping.
F. Security Layer: AES-256-GCM encryption and HMAC-SHA256 authentication protect emotional data, while a honeypot defends the IoT infrastructure.
06
Data Analysis

Analytical Framework

Routine Analysis

Uses mean and standard deviation to differentiate normal variation from major routine changes. Tolerance model: μ + max(1.5σ, 45min).

Focus Scoring

Focus = w₁(1 − B) + w₂G + w₃F + w₄E. Analyzes EAR, gaze alignment, fixation duration, and environmental context.

Stress Validation

Evaluates CNN-LSTM outputs against the Russell Circumplex Model. Validated via confusion matrices, precision, and temporal consistency.

Cybersecurity Evaluation

Honeypot effectiveness measured via True Positive Rate (TPR), False Positive Rate (FPR), and Isolation Forest anomaly scores.
